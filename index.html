Croissants are more French than Austrian; they originated in Vienna, Austria, as a crescent-shaped pastry called kipferl, and were later popularized and transformed into their modern, flaky form by French bakers.
A different kind of lamination: Croissant dough is different from puff pastry. The unique lamination process for croissants involves layering butter into the dough through a series of folds and rolls, creating countless thin, delicate layers that puff up during baking.
